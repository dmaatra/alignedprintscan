Common Notary Documents

Examples of common general notary work. Final service is confirmed after your document type, signer needs, travel, and timing are reviewed.

Acknowledgements
Used when a signer confirms they willingly signed a document, such as deeds, certain contracts, or other formal records.
Jurats
Used when a signer must swear or affirm that the contents of a document are true before signing in front of the notary.
Affidavits
Written sworn statements that often require a jurat and are commonly used for personal, legal, or administrative matters.
Power of Attorney
Documents authorizing another person to act on someone’s behalf, often requiring careful review of signer readiness and identification.
Consent Forms
Common for travel, medical, school, or guardianship situations where a properly notarized signature may be requested.
Sworn Statements
Declarations or statements that may require oath-based notarization depending on how the document is prepared.
